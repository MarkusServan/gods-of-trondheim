The Sámi People in Trondheim

Sámi presence in a modern city

About Sámi culture

The Sámi are Norway's indigenous people and have inhabited the area known as Sápmi for thousands of years. Their culture is rich in traditions tied to reindeer herding, joik (traditional song), handicraft (duodji), and a deep connection to nature.

Trondheim is an important city for Sámi history and is often referred to as a central meeting place. We wanted to explore the city and see how Sámi culture is integrated into it.

🔍 Our fieldwork

Throughout this project, we set out to explore the presence of Sámi culture in Trondheim. We joined a Sámi city walk, attended a public debate and experienced a contemporary art exhibition. We also had the privilege of interviewing Sámi individuals about their lives and identity in the city. Through these encounters, we gained a nuanced understanding of how Sámi culture exists and finds expression in Trondheim today.
